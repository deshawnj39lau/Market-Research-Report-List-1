<p><p>The antifreeze automobile market comprises various types, including calcium chloride (CaCl2), methanol (CH3OH), ethanol (C2H5OH), ethylene glycol (C2H4(OH)2), and glycerol (C3H5(OH)3). CaCl2 is often used for lower freezing points, while methanol and ethanol serve as effective solvents and antifreeze agents. Ethylene glycol is the most common for engine cooling due to its excellent thermal properties. Glycerol, a biodegradable option, is increasingly favored for eco-friendly applications. Each type serves unique performance and environmental needs in automotive cooling systems.</p></p>
